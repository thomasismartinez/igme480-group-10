Thomas

In order to allow the user to create grafitti, I used html5 canvas to create a simple drawing, that then saves the user's drawing as a png. That png is then inserted into the AR.js scene as an image, positioned in front of the houses halls.

Presley

My main contribution was integrating Firebase Firestore into our AR graffiti project to enable saving and loading graffiti artwork. I implemented the functionality to upload the user's canvas drawing as a base64-encoded image to Firestore when they clicked "Done," and also created a system to retrieve those saved images and display them in the AR scene. This allowed graffiti to persist between sessions and made the experience feel more collaborative and interactive.
